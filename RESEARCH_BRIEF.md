# Czech Corporate Loans Market — Research Brief

This document distills 20+ research prompts into a single reusable brief.
Hand it to a competent analyst (or a frontier LLM with web search) to reproduce
or extend the tracker. The brief is grouped by mission, scope, search lenses,
sources, methods, and known names — in roughly the order the work should run.

---

## 1. Mission

Reconstruct the **real Czech corporate lending market** for the last five
years (calendar 2021 through Q1 2026) — not just the publicly-syndicated tip
of the iceberg. Output is a single live tracker (HTML on GitHub Pages) plus a
companion methodology document that explains, deal by deal, how each entry
was uncovered. Confidence labelling is mandatory.

The deliverable should be defensible to a Czech corporate banker — i.e. it
should look like the universe a senior originator at ČSOB / KB / UniCredit
CZ&SK / ČS would recognise as their actual market.

---

## 2. Universe definition (inclusion criteria)

A transaction qualifies when **all** of the following hold:

**(a) Geographic / corporate scope** — at least one of:
- Borrower domiciled in the Czech Republic, including SPVs holding Czech operating assets.
- Czech parent of a foreign group raising debt at HoldCo or OpCo level.
- Czech bank as lender or advisor on a cross-border deal where the underlying obligor is non-CZ but the Czech leg is material.
- Czech exporter as supply-side beneficiary of a buyer-credit / supplier-credit structure (counterparty borrows; the deal is generated by Czech industry — Iveco-buses-to-Ivory-Coast pattern).

**(b) Size threshold** — reported or reasonably estimated facility size **≥ EUR 30 million equivalent** (1 EUR ≈ 25 CZK; ≥ CZK 750m as the koruna threshold).

**(c) Type scope** — corporate term loans, revolving credit, acquisition / LBO finance, refinancings, sustainability-linked loans, project & infrastructure finance, export finance (buyer credit / supplier credit), aircraft & rolling-stock financing, real estate development & investment finance, intermediated lines from supranationals (EIB / EBRD) on-lent by CZ banks.
**Excluded**: pure bond issues, IPO/equity, intra-group loans, non-strategic working-capital lines below threshold.

**(d) Time scope** — calendar 2021-01-01 through Q1 2026.

**(e) Threshold for company UBO scope** — borrower / obligor (or its CZ leg) had revenue **≥ CZK 1 billion** in the year before the deal. (For SPV obligors, look through to the underlying business.)

deal will often be found through more than one lens; the redundancy is a
feature.

Walk every deal type (syndicated/club, LBO, refi, CRE, export finance,
project/infra, intermediated) and search for the largest CZ examples in each.
This is the easiest lens but produces the most press-release bias and skews
toward recent years.

## 4. Source taxonomy (where to look)

Sources are organised by what they reveal — not who publishes them.
Effective deal reconstruction uses ≥3 categories per deal.

### A. Czech statutory & regulatory disclosures
- **Sbírka listin** (justice.cz) — every a.s. and most s.r.o. file annual financial statements; the **"Bankovní úvěry" / "Závazky vůči úvěrovým institucím"** note lists each facility, lender, currency, balance, maturity. Single richest source. Search by IČO.
- **Notářská komora ČR / pledge of business interests registry** — pledged shareholdings = lender = pledgee.
- **Katastr nemovitostí** (cuzk.cz) — every CRE financing leaves zástavní právo on the LV; pledgee is the lender.
- **ČNB credit register (CRU)** — sectoral aggregate statistics for sense-checking quantum.
- **Insolvenční rejstřík** (isir.justice.cz) — when a borrower fails, all creditors register claims with face value and identity. Reveals the syndicate ex-post (Bohemia Energy 2021, Liberty Ostrava etc.).

### B. Capital markets & rating
- **Bond prospectuses** — issuers like CPIPG, EPH, EP Infrastructure, ČEZ, NET4GAS, PPF Arena 1, Sazka/Allwyn, Energo-Pro, Skoda Group, Dr.Max, KKCG, CSG, CETIN, Penta Hospitals tabulate their existing bank facilities under "Description of Indebtedness". Listings: Bourse Luxembourg, Euronext Dublin, Vienna SE, Prague SE.
- **Rating agency reports** (Moody's, S&P, Fitch, Scope) — issuer credit reports list "Key indebtedness".
- **Annual reports** at group AND entity level — group shows aggregate, entity-level Czech filings break by lender.

### C. State / supranational / public-sector
- **EGAP** (egap.cz) — annual report tables list large insured contracts; press for individual ECA-backed deals.
- **Česká exportní banka (ČEB)** — direct-loan annual report; case studies (Aero Vodochody / Iveco / Skoda Group / Doosan Skoda Power / Agrostroj).
- **EIB project database** (eib.org/projects) — searchable by country = Czechia. Direct loans + intermediated lines.
- **EBRD Project Summary Documents** (ebrd.com) — every CZ project lists co-financiers.
- **NRB (Národní rozvojová banka)** — state-development bank; on-lent EIB lines.
- **Public procurement** (vestnikverejnychzakazek.cz, ted.europa.eu) — financial-advisor mandates leak deals 6-12 months ahead.
- **Modernization Fund** — RES+ programme for renewables.

### D. Sectoral asset-level
- **Energetický regulační úřad (ERÚ)** — license holders → project finance trail.
- **ÚHOS merger decisions** — control-change clearance describes consideration & sometimes financing.
- **Drážní úřad** (Czech Rail Vehicle Register) — rolling-stock liens (RegioJet/SGEF, ČD/EIB, Skoda export deliveries).
- **ÚCL (Czech Aircraft Register)** — aircraft mortgages (Smartwings, ČSA, Travel Service).

### E. Indirect / inferential
- **M&A databases** — Mergermarket, Bureau van Dijk Zephyr, S&P Capital IQ, Pitchbook (Forbes-tracked), Mergr, Tracxn.
- **Law-firm "Deals" pages** — Dentons, A&O Shearman, White & Case, Clifford Chance, Linklaters, Kinstellar, PRK Partners, Havel & Partners, Baker McKenzie, CMS, Schoenherr, Norton Rose Fulbright, Greenberg Traurig, Latham & Watkins, Kirkland & Ellis, BBH.
- **Trade press** — Property Forum, CIJ Europe, EuropaProperty, CEE Real Estate Matters, CEE Legal Matters, Defense News, FlightGlobal, Railway Gazette, Reuters Czech, E15, Hospodářské noviny, Forbes.cz, businessinfo.cz, ihned.cz.
- **Lender-bank disclosures** — ČSOB, KB, ČS, UniCredit CZ&SK, Raiffeisenbank, Erste, J&T Banka quarterly investor presentations + Pillar 3.
- **LinkedIn deal-team posts** with phrases "advised on" / "led syndication" / "acted as MLA" / "MLAB".

### F. Forensic indirect
- **CNB monetary statistics** — aggregate moves can flag a single big drawdown.
- **BIS Locational Banking Statistics** — foreign claims on CZ.
- **CZK/EUR cross-currency basis swap volumes** — proxy for big EUR borrowing.

## 5. Triangulation methods

When direct disclosure is missing, reconstruct via ≥2 of:

### Loan size estimation
- **LBO triangulation** — announced acquisition price × 4-5x net debt/EBITDA (CEE benchmark).
- **Bond-prospectus residual** — total reported indebtedness minus bonds = bank stack.
- **AR delta** — Y-o-Y change in "Bank loans" note net of identified repayments = new drawings.
- **Pledge-registry maximum-amount field** ("maximální zajišťovaná částka") typically = 110-125% of facility face.
- **Pillar 3 single-counterparty exposure caps** — upper bound from lender side.
- **Comparable-deal benchmarking** — similar CZ borrower × similar leverage × recent transaction.

### Lender identification
- Pledge / mortgage registry — pledgee = lender (most reliable).
- Insolvency claim register — ex-post syndicate reveal.
- Bond prospectus "Description of Indebtedness" — bank list by name.
- Law-firm "advised lenders" announcements — even if borrower silent.
- Signing-day photography / LinkedIn — banks identifiable from logos/badges.
- Credit-insurance underwriter (EGAP / ČEB / KUKE / OEKB) — names the insured bank.
- Borrower's own annual report breakdown at entity level.

## 6. Confidence framework

| Label | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Confirmed** | Explicit press release, regulator filing, supranational disclosure, OR law-firm "advised on" announcement naming borrower + amount + ≥1 lender. |
| **Likely** | ≥2 independent indirect signals point to the same deal; quantum bounded; specific lender or amount may be approximate. |
| **Inferred** | Single signal or estimation; would require Sbírka-listin pull or insider verification to upgrade to Likely. |

## 7. Output specification

The tracker is a **single self-contained HTML file** (`index.html`) deployable
to GitHub Pages. Key UX requirements:

- Header: title + sub-line stating scope ("Syndicated, LBO, refinancing, CRE, export finance, project & infrastructure financings — 2021-Q1 2026").
- **Stats panel** — count of filtered deals, EUR-equivalent aggregate, breakdown by deal type.
- **Filter chips** by deal type (All, Syndicated, Acquisition/LBO, Refinancing, Real Estate, Export Finance, Project/Infra, Intermediated).
- **Year filter chips** (All, 2026, 2025, 2024, 2023, 2022, 2021) — the year distribution should be roughly balanced across years; heavy 2024 skew is a research-bias signal.
- **Sortable Grid.js table** with columns: Borrower / Type / Conf. (badge: Confirmed = green, Likely = yellow, Inferred = grey) / Amount / Date / Lenders / Purpose / Source link.
- Filter selections **persist in `localStorage`** so the user's last filter is remembered.
- **Light mode**, color-scheme: light, system fonts, no JS dependencies beyond Grid.js (CDN: jsdelivr v5.0.2 with integrity hash).
- Single file — no build step, no module loader.

Companion file: `research-methodology.docx` documenting source taxonomy,
triangulation methods, and per-deal sourcing notes for the inferred / likely
entries.

Repo structure:

```

├── index.html               # the tracker

├── README.md                # one-pager
├── RESEARCH_BRIEF.md        # this file
```

## 8. Workflow phases

The order matters — earlier phases build the universe; later phases fill
gaps.

### Phase 1 — Press-release sweep (1-2 days)
Walk Lens 1 (deal type) using Czech-bank lender announcements + law-firm
deal pages + issuer press releases. Yield: ~30-40 deals in the easiest
recent-2024-2025 cohort.

### Phase 2 — UBO walk (2-3 days)
Walk Lens 2 (UBOs) for each major family-office. Yield: another ~30-40
deals, with material 2021-2022 backfill. The CPI Property Group
IMMOFINANZ/S IMMO bridges (€2.5bn + €1.25bn) and the CETIN €1.65bn facility
typically only emerge here.

### Phase 3 — Fund mapping (2 days)
Walk Lens 3 (global + CEE PE/credit funds). Surfaces the buy-side bank
syndicate names that don't appear under borrower-name search (Carlyle/Meopta
6-bank syndicate, FTMO/UniCredit-led 6-bank club, Blackstone/Contera €470m
buy).

### Phase 4 — Sector / specific-name backfill (2-3 days)
Walk Lens 4 against known company list. Plug remaining gaps.

### Phase 5 — Sbírka listin / katastr / insolvency scrape (engineering)
Programmatic — the highest-yield phase but the most technical. Selenium /
Playwright on or.justice.cz, batched by IČO list (~700 obligors). PDF →
text → regex on "Bankovní úvěry" note. Yield: 80-120 additional deals,
heavily weighted to 2021-2023 backfill.

### Phase 6 — Continuous monitoring
RSS / Google Alerts / LinkedIn Sales Navigator saved searches. Webhook
on justice.cz for tracked IČO universe.

## 11. Honest caveats — known limitations

1. **Year-distribution skew toward 2024** is research bias not market reality. Google indexing favours recent press releases. True distribution is closer to 12 / 18 / 22 / 28 / 25 / 5 across 2021-2026.
2. **2021 + 2022 are systematically under-sampled** in any web-only build. The CPIPG bridges (€3.75bn aggregate) only emerge through UBO-walk + bond-prospectus reads.
3. **Tomáš Chrenek group** (Třinec / Moravia Steel / AGEL / Metalimex) is the single largest blind spot. ~€1bn+ of likely bank facilities are not in any English-language source. Sbírka listin needed.
4. **Babiš / Agrofert** corporate-level facilities are similar — sell-side only via Kaprain.
5. **Manufacturing-MNCs** (Škoda Auto, Hyundai MMC, Toyota MMC, Foxconn, Plzeňský Prazdroj) almost certainly have CZ-level facilities but parent-financed structure obscures them.
6. **Bond-financed deals** — many CZ corporates use bonds rather than bank loans (Energo-Pro €750m green Eurobond 2025, KKCG CZK 6bn 2024, Dr.Max CZK 5bn 2023, CETIN €500m 2022, CSG bonds before IPO). These are excluded per scope but acknowledged.
